Places to Explore 🗺

From right around the corner to a short train ride away

📍 Right Around You
Legnano — The Local City
🏙 Town · Shopping · History
Famous for the 1176 Battle of Legnano. Visit the Castello di Legnano, stroll the pedestrian center, enjoy great cafés and restaurants.
🚍 Z627 · ~15 min🚃 5 min by train
Parco del Ticino
🌿 Nature · Cycling · River
A vast regional nature park along the Ticino river — cycling, walking, herons and kingfishers. Rent a bike in Turbigo or Magenta for a scenic ride.
🚴 Cycling🚌 Bus to Cuggiono or Turbigo
Busto Arsizio
🏛 Town · History · Shopping
Elegant architecture, great shopping. See Santa Maria di Piazza and the textile museum — the area is famous for its fabric industry.
🚃 15 min by train🚍 30 min by bus
🚡 23 Marzo — Gita Scolastica a Bergamo
📅 School trip on March 23rd! Bergamo is one of the most beautiful cities in Lombardy — a Venetian walled upper city (Città Alta) perched on a hill above the modern lower city. UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2017.

Bergamo — Città Alta 🏰
🏛 UNESCO · Venetian Walls · Baroque · Medieval
One of the most beautiful città murate (walled cities) in Italy — 6 km of Venetian Renaissance walls, baroque churches, and medieval towers. Stroll the Piazza Vecchia, climb the Torre del Campanone for panoramic views, and explore the historic streets. The Orto Botanico and the Accademia Carrara (one of Italy's finest art galleries) are nearby.

Palocapa (Istituto Tecnico Pietro Secco Suardo / Palocapa) is one of Bergamo's historic technical institutes — interesting to compare with IIS Mendel!
La Viscontea is a historic building in the upper city, testament to Bergamo's Visconti and Venetian past.
🚃 ~50 min from Milano Centrale🚡 Funicolare from station

Easter Break Trips 🐣

Italy is YOUR oyster during the holidays! All reachable by train from Milan.

🌷 Easter 2026 falls on April 5th. Italian schools typically have about 10 days off around Easter — plenty of time for amazing train adventures! Book tickets in advance (especially Frecciarossa) — Easter is peak season and trains fill up fast.

Bologna — La Grassa
Emilia-Romagna · "The Fat One" · UNESCO porticoes
Bologna is one of Italy's most underrated cities and a food lover's paradise. Famous for ragù alla bolognese (the real one!), mortadella, and tortellini. Stunning medieval architecture with 40 km of porticoes, two iconic leaning towers, and a vibrant university-town energy. A perfect one-day or overnight trip!
🚆 From Milano Centrale: Frecciarossa or Italo — ~1 hour direct!
📍 Arrive at: Bologna Centrale (central station, everything walkable)
🎫 Fare: from ~€15–25 if booked in advance
🕐 Frequency: trains every 20–30 min throughout the day
🍝 Best food in Italy 🏛 Medieval towers ⚡ 1h direct
Must-see: Due Torri (two leaning towers) · Piazza Maggiore · Basilica di San Petronio · Mercato di Mezzo (food market!) · MAMbo modern art museum
Must-eat: tortellini in brodo · crescentine fritte · mortadella · gelato at Sorbetteria Castiglione
🚄 Book on Trenitalia → 🔴 Book on Italo → 🗺 Map →

Genova — La Superba
Liguria · Caruggi · Pesto · Sea
Genova is a city of dramatic contrasts — a UNESCO historic center with narrow medieval alleys (caruggi), a stunning waterfront with an aquarium, and the birthplace of Christopher Columbus and pesto! An authentic, gritty, gorgeous city that tourists often skip — don't make that mistake!
🚆 From Milano Centrale: Intercity or EC — ~1h 30min direct
📍 Arrive at: Genova Piazza Principe or Genova Brignole
🎫 Fare: from ~€12–20 depending on train type
🕐 Frequency: trains every ~60 min (being upgraded to every 60 min from Dec 2025)
⚓ Maritime history 🌿 Pesto birthplace 🐟 Best seafood 🏛 UNESCO center
Must-see: Caruggi (medieval alleys) · Acquario di Genova (one of Europe's largest!) · Porto Antico · Palazzo Ducale · Boccadasse fishing village
Must-eat: focaccia genovese · pesto al mortaio · farinata · frisceu (fried dough) · pansotti pasta
🚄 Book on Trenitalia → 🗺 Map →

Cinque Terre
Liguria · UNESCO · 5 Cliffside Villages · Sea
Five impossibly beautiful villages clinging to cliffs above the turquoise Ligurian Sea — Riomaggiore, Manarola, Corniglia, Vernazza, Monterosso. Colorful houses, vineyard trails, and the famous Via dell'Amore coastal path. One of Italy's most iconic destinations — best visited on a weekday or in the morning to beat the crowds. Plan for at least 2 days to see all five properly!
🚆 Step 1 — Milano Centrale → La Spezia: Frecciarossa (direct or via Genova) ~2h – 2h 30min
🚆 Step 2 — La Spezia → Villages: Cinque Terre Express regional — every 30 min, 7–20 min per village
📍 Villages from La Spezia: Riomaggiore (7 min) · Manarola (10 min) · Corniglia (14 min) · Vernazza (20 min) · Monterosso (25 min)
🎫 Fare: Milano→La Spezia from ~€25–40 · Cinque Terre card (all villages, unlimited trains): ~€20/day
💡 Tip: Buy the Cinque Terre Card at La Spezia station — includes all local trains between villages!
🏘 5 villages 🌊 Crystal sea 🥾 Hiking trails 📸 UNESCO · Most photographed
Must-do: Walk the Via dell'Amore (Riomaggiore–Manarola) · Swim at Monterosso beach · Hike Vernazza–Monterosso trail · Sunset aperitivo in any piazza
Must-eat: anchovies · focaccia · pesto trofie · sciacchetrà (local sweet wine) · fried calamari at the port
⚠️ Book accommodations VERY early — Easter week fills up months in advance!

Florence — Firenze
Tuscany · Renaissance · The Uffizi · Michelangelo
The cradle of the Renaissance — arguably the most art-dense city on Earth. Michelangelo's David, Botticelli's Birth of Venus, Brunelleschi's Dome — all here. Plus the most beautiful historic center in Italy (UNESCO), world-class food, and Tuscany's rolling hills visible from the hilltop Piazzale Michelangelo. A day trip is possible but two days is ideal!
🚆 From Milano Centrale: Frecciarossa — ~1h 45min direct!
📍 Arrive at: Firenze Santa Maria Novella (right in the center)
🎫 Fare: from ~€20–35 if booked in advance
🕐 Frequency: trains every 20–30 min throughout the day
🎨 Renaissance capital ⚡ 1h 45min direct 🏛 Uffizi · David · Duomo 🍷 Best Chianti
Must-see: Uffizi Gallery (book online!) · Accademia (Michelangelo's David — book online!) · Brunelleschi's Dome (climb it!) · Piazzale Michelangelo at sunset · Ponte Vecchio · Oltrarno neighborhood
Must-eat: bistecca alla fiorentina · lampredotto (street food) · ribollita soup · gelato at Gelateria dei Neri · Chianti wine
⚠️ Book Uffizi and Accademia tickets online weeks in advance — Easter queues are enormous!
🚄 Book on Trenitalia → 🔴 Book on Italo → 🎨 Uffizi tickets →

Verona — Città dell'Amore
Veneto · Romeo & Juliet · UNESCO · Arena
Verona is one of northern Italy's most beautiful cities — a perfectly preserved Roman and medieval center, UNESCO-listed, with the famous Roman Arena (still used for opera), Juliet's Balcony, and the most romantic atmosphere you can imagine. Easily combinable with a morning or afternoon at nearby Lake Garda!
🚆 From Milano Centrale: Frecciarossa or Italo — ~1h 10min direct!
📍 Arrive at: Verona Porta Nuova (15 min walk or bus to center)
🎫 Fare: from ~€15–30 if booked in advance
🕐 Frequency: trains every 30 min throughout the day
💡 Tip: Combine with Lake Garda — Sirmione is only 40 min by bus from Verona station!
❤️ Romeo & Juliet ⚡ 1h 10min direct 🏟 Roman Arena 🏛 UNESCO center
Must-see: Arena di Verona (1st century Roman amphitheater, still used for opera!) · Casa di Giulietta & balcony · Piazza delle Erbe · Castel San Pietro (hilltop, stunning views) · Ponte Pietra (Roman bridge)
Must-eat: risotto all'Amarone · pastissada de caval · pandoro (it was invented in Verona!) · Soave & Valpolicella wines
🚄 Book on Trenitalia → 🔴 Book on Italo → 🗺 Map →

Lago di Garda — Sirmione & Beyond
Italy's largest lake · Sirmione · Desenzano · Malcesine
Lake Garda is Italy's largest and most popular lake — crystal-clear water, lemon groves, olive trees, dramatic cliffs in the north, and gentle shores in the south. Sirmione is the jewel: a medieval castle town on a narrow peninsula jutting into the lake, with Roman ruins and thermal baths. Stunning at Easter when spring flowers are everywhere!
🚆 Option 1 — Via Desenzano (quickest to Sirmione):
  Milano Centrale → Desenzano del Garda/Rivoltella ~40–50 min (Frecciarossa or Regionale)
  Then 🚌 Bus (line 483 ATV) Desenzano station → Sirmione ~20 min · ~€2.50
  🎫 Train from ~€8–20 depending on type

🚆 Option 2 — Via Verona (combine both cities!):
  Milano → Verona (1h 10min) → Bus to Sirmione (40 min) or other lake towns
  Visit Verona in the morning, lake in the afternoon — perfect day trip!

🚌 Within Lake Garda: ATV buses connect all lakeside towns — check atv.verona.it
🏰 Sirmione castle 🌊 Italy's largest lake 🛁 Thermal baths 🍋 Lemon groves
Sirmione must-see: Castello Scaligero (medieval castle right on the water) · Grotte di Catullo (Roman ruins on the peninsula tip) · Terme di Sirmione (thermal spa) · Walk the full peninsula
Other lake towns: Desenzano (lively, walkable from train station) · Lazise (prettiest medieval village) · Malcesine in the north (cable car up Monte Baldo with Alpine panorama!)
Must-eat: freshwater fish (lavarello, carpione) · olive oil from the lake · Lugana white wine · lemon everything
⚠️ Sirmione is very crowded at Easter — arrive early in the morning!

🗓 Planning Your Easter Trips

Book train tickets ASAP! Frecciarossa and Italo tickets are cheapest 1–3 months in advance. Use Trenitalia or Italo directly — or compare on Trainline.

🎒 Suggested order of priority: Cinque Terre (most booking-sensitive, plan 2 days) → Florence (book museums now) → Verona + Lake Garda (perfect combo in one day!) → Bologna (easiest, can be spontaneous) → Genova (flexible, trains frequent)

🇮🇹 Pasquetta (Easter Monday) is a national holiday — many places close in the morning but open in the afternoon. Great day for nature walks or the seaside!
